03 — Historical Context

How Did We
Get Here?

Understanding today's crisis requires understanding India's history. Minority discrimination did not appear overnight — it is the product of decades of political tension, social inequality, and systemic neglect.

Post-Partition — 1947

The partition of British India into India and Pakistan created deep religious fault lines that shaped communal relations for generations. Violence and displacement left wounds that never fully healed.

1992 — Babri Masjid

The demolition of the Babri Masjid mosque triggered widespread communal riots across India, exposing deep religious tensions and the fragility of minority protections under pressure.

2002 — Gujarat Riots

Over 1,000 people — predominantly Muslims — were killed in Gujarat. The riots became a defining moment in India's minority rights crisis, with widespread criticism of government inaction.

2019 — CAA Controversy

The Citizenship Amendment Act, which excluded Muslims from a path to citizenship, sparked nationwide protests and drew international condemnation from human rights organizations.

2023–2025 — Rising Crisis

Verified hate speech events rose 97% between 2023 and 2025. Social media amplification, political rhetoric, and institutional failures created the perfect storm for the crisis we face today.

04 — The Issue

The Scale
of the Problem

This is not a distant political issue. It is a human crisis — unfolding across every state, every city, every community in India right now.

↑
The Numbers
1,318

Verified hate speech events in 2025 alone. A 13% rise from 2024 and a staggering 97% rise since 2023. Muslims and Christians are the primary targets.

✕
The Silence
87%

Of hate crimes are never reported. Victims fear retaliation, distrust police — who are sometimes perpetrators themselves — and believe reporting will change nothing.

The Law

Sections 153A and 295A of the IPC prohibit inciting religious hatred — but are frequently weaponized against minorities themselves. India has no standalone hate crime law.

▦
Social Media
950M

Internet users in India in 2025. WhatsApp's encrypted groups and algorithmic amplification allow hate to spread faster than truth — to nearly every home in the country.

◉
Global Rank
#10

India ranks 10th most dangerous country for Christians, despite being the 4th largest economy in the world. Economic power has not translated into safety for minorities.

⊞
Root Causes

Political polarization, caste hierarchies, nationalist rhetoric, economic scapegoating, and decades of institutional neglect have created the conditions for this crisis to grow unchecked.
